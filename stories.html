Stories

My name is Vishwas Kushwaha, and I'm a 3rd-year mechanical engineering undergraduate. This is my story of how I realized my interest in tennis and worked my way to win 6 national tournaments. Initially, I was not a huge fan of tennis, never really knew much about it. When I got shifted to the Railway Colony in Allahabad, there were three tennis courts just beside the badminton hall there. I had never seen tennis being played in person, so I went to check out what it was? And that was my first encounter with the sport. I saw children my age (I was 15 at that time) ...doing rallies with the coach, and it looked pretty astonishing. That's when I told myself I need to play this. So the next day there I was there, standing on the court with my Dixon tennis racket. My coach asked me if I had any experience in tennis, to which I replied no. He put in the second batch, which was for beginners. So I trained there for two months, from learning how to hold a racket to knowing the rules, techniques, and icons of tennis (BTW, I'm a huge Roger Fan). After these two months of induction, I got promoted to the first batch where the best players (players who wanted to pursue tennis as a career) used to play. My coach made me play with 2 of them who were younger than me. I thought it would be super easy but got crushed 6-0 by both of them. My coach told me post-match that I need to reach their fitness level and precision if I ever wanted to defeat them. From here, started the actual work. I started training every day of the week from 4-6 PM. It started with running 30 minutes non-stop, then line touching and sprints, standard in all sports. There were many other drills distributed all over the week, which we had to complete before touching our rackets. We used to have practice matches on weekends, but before that, we had to run ten rounds of the cricket stadium (approx 6-7 km) with 2 kg weights on each ankle. Seniors had to play matches with 0.5 kg wrist weights on. The coach did this to prepare us for the mental fight we had to face when playing long games in harsh conditions. And it did pay off. My first national tournament was at La Martiniere College, Lucknow, when I was 16, and I was playing in the under 18 singles and doubles category. I lost in the quarter-finals of singles but won the doubles with my partner Aman Kumar, currently ranked 24th in India. I was quite happy with my result, but my coach was not. He increased the intensity of drills for everyone. Two months after that, I played my 2nd open national tournament in Indore and won my first national trophy and prize money. Big moment for me. I used this money to buy shoes and rackets because, in tennis, the racket is your sword and shoes, your armor (although I wanted to buy some fancy rackets and shoes to show off, I was too young for this racket-sword philosophy). After this, I played many tournaments, of which I won some and lost many, but it was an amazing experience. I stopped playing tennis after my 10th boards and switched to badminton because I needed the energy to study, which was usually gone after my tennis practice. After joining college, I play tennis occasionally, whenever I'm home with my friends. I don't have the same level of game I used to have, but I'm good enough to hit the ball on the opposite side of the court somehow.
