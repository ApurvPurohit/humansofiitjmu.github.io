Stories

My name is Jay Narayana, and I am from the Begusarai district in Bihar. I come from a farming background; my father is a teacher by occupation but does farming out of interest. I also liked farming, and in my childhood days, I went to the fields with my father. I enjoyed sports and played lots of them till high school. In my 11th standard, I decided to focus on my studies. Although I was good in academics, I had little idea about IITs and JEE and was not that interested. I used to go to a regular coaching center for my studies. One day, I saw an advertisement... that some famous coaching institutes for JEE preparations that provided free teaching, food, and lodging, were conducting scholarship tests. I decided to give it a shot and started preparing for them. I came 5th out of 3200 aspiring students and was also selected for Anand Kumar Sir's famous Super 30 batch. This was a major motivation for me to start preparing for JEE. From here, I began my preparations. After two years of extensive coaching, uncountable exams and mocks, and endless sacrifices, I made it to IIT Jammu as an Electrical Engineering undergraduate. When I joined college, it was a different world altogether. We were completely free and could live life by our rules. In my first year of engineering, I enjoyed this new unrestricted environment - the college life, activities, and everything happening in my life to the fullest. The academics weren't that burdensome, and the fun was unlimited. As I progressed in my course, every night, I would contemplate my day's routine, activities, and honestly speaking, I found myself in dissatisfaction. When I went home for summer vacations, I tried doing a lot of different things. I was trying to understand my purpose, what I was made for, and what kind of work I enjoyed doing. Coming from Bihar, my parents also had the same dream every parent has - to clear the UPSC exams and secure a good government administrative position. The professors in my college were also study-oriented and focussed primarily on academics. However, I was very average performing in college, getting 6-7 pointers in each semester. In my third semester, I came in contact with one of the project engineers at IIT Jammu, Pranjal Sir, who introduced me to the concept of entrepreneurship and motivated me to explore it. I also liked the idea and started exploring this amazing field. For one year, I was studying only entrepreneurship. This even affected my grades in those semesters, but all of it seemed worth the sacrifice. During this period, I was the coordinator of the Arts Club in college. There were some meetings regarding the institute logo with Director sir. On 13th April 2018 (the date is still in my head), I pitched a faint idea of a cafe that I wanted to open to him. I was in my 4th semester at that time. His initial response was that I should talk to the students and people on the campus and find out what was actually wanted - what the current facilities were unable to provide? I brainstormed for a long time with my friends and some juniors on this topic - the canteen facility in our college, the food delivery system and everything in between. I also started working on some other ideas parallelly along with this one. During this time, there was a seminar on entrepreneurship conducted in our college by Team Startup India. Along with two of my juniors - Vishwas and Tushar, I was working on an idea related to farming during that time. We decided to pitch the concept when they talk opened for questions and ideas. Our idea was to remove any third-party involvement between the farmer and consumer via supply chain optimization, middlemen cost-cutting, and intelligent use of technology. We laid out a clear-cut strategy to provide a higher than the current selling price to the farmer and lower than the current cost price to the consumer. The panel liked our idea and considered us for a national event they were conducting. After this, there was a telephonic interview round, in which we qualified and got selected for the finals in October 2018. We bagged the 3rd position among 580 teams in the finals for our idea - which was an unbelievable achievement. This served as a motivation booster for us - we got results much better than we had ever expected. Also, we got incubation offers from 2 government-funded farming industries. We have been working on the idea and are gaining more and more insight in this vast field of farming and agriculture, which constitutes about 40% of our GDP. My experience with opening a full-fledged cafe in our college has also been full of ups and downs. I had to make uncountable visits to our college's office and admin department. There were numerous committees formed, including people from all departments of IIT Jammu, and I had to bring all of them on the same page. I can loosely say that in some sense, I had to convince the entire college that my idea was feasible and worth a chance. This was a very tough and non-rewarding period. After many failed attempts, inconclusive meetings, and conflicting opinions, came a day that changed everything. When the country had gone in Lockdown, and there was a scarcity of food services in our college, my proposal got accepted. I had been going to the admin department for a long time now. It was a regular Sunday afternoon, and after some final discussions, I was given the official permission to set things up. I promised to set up the cafe in 2 weeks. We had to bring in everything, from supplies to the seating arrangements, decorations, and everything necessary, in just 14 days, so we started that very evening. From a space near the admin department that was of no use to a fully functional cafe - we did it all in just 14 days and 14 sleepless nights. I have named the cafe, Cafe 4teeN's - "Apna Adda." It was inaugurated on 14th September 2020 by one of the founding members of IIT Jammu - Pandya Sir, and we have not stopped working a single day since then. It has been a great experience, and we're still improving and getting used to the hardships and struggles. After judging our quality of service, we even got an offer from Director sir to open a subsidiary outlet in the Paloura campus, which I accepted as soon as I heard it! It has been a roller coaster ride since Cafe 4teeN's has started. Currently, the only dream I have for our cafe is to improve our reach. We want to target other educational institutions - schools, universities, markets with the same spirit and provide quality service to our customers. Profit is not a priority since this is the time to establish our business and build our brand, which will require a lot of rough days and nights. The days when we can sit back and relax are yet to come, but we are working hard for it. I am working with the motto that all our dreams can come true if we dare to pursue them.

My name is Vishwas Kushwaha, and I'm a 3rd-year mechanical engineering undergraduate. This is my story of how I realized my interest in tennis and worked my way to win 6 national tournaments. Initially, I was not a huge fan of tennis, never really knew much about it. When I got shifted to the Railway Colony in Allahabad, there were three tennis courts just beside the badminton hall there. I had never seen tennis being played in person, so I went to check out what it was? And that was my first encounter with the sport. I saw children my age (I was 15 at that time) ...doing rallies with the coach, and it looked pretty astonishing. That's when I told myself I need to play this. So the next day there I was there, standing on the court with my Dixon tennis racket. My coach asked me if I had any experience in tennis, to which I replied no. He put in the second batch, which was for beginners. So I trained there for two months, from learning how to hold a racket to knowing the rules, techniques, and icons of tennis (BTW, I'm a huge Roger Fan). After these two months of induction, I got promoted to the first batch where the best players (players who wanted to pursue tennis as a career) used to play. My coach made me play with 2 of them who were younger than me. I thought it would be super easy but got crushed 6-0 by both of them. My coach told me post-match that I need to reach their fitness level and precision if I ever wanted to defeat them. From here, started the actual work. I started training every day of the week from 4-6 PM. It started with running 30 minutes non-stop, then line touching and sprints, standard in all sports. There were many other drills distributed all over the week, which we had to complete before touching our rackets. We used to have practice matches on weekends, but before that, we had to run ten rounds of the cricket stadium (approx 6-7 km) with 2 kg weights on each ankle. Seniors had to play matches with 0.5 kg wrist weights on. The coach did this to prepare us for the mental fight we had to face when playing long games in harsh conditions. And it did pay off. My first national tournament was at La Martiniere College, Lucknow, when I was 16, and I was playing in the under 18 singles and doubles category. I lost in the quarter-finals of singles but won the doubles with my partner Aman Kumar, currently ranked 24th in India. I was quite happy with my result, but my coach was not. He increased the intensity of drills for everyone. Two months after that, I played my 2nd open national tournament in Indore and won my first national trophy and prize money. Big moment for me. I used this money to buy shoes and rackets because, in tennis, the racket is your sword and shoes, your armor (although I wanted to buy some fancy rackets and shoes to show off, I was too young for this racket-sword philosophy). After this, I played many tournaments, of which I won some and lost many, but it was an amazing experience. I stopped playing tennis after my 10th boards and switched to badminton because I needed the energy to study, which was usually gone after my tennis practice. After joining college, I play tennis occasionally, whenever I'm home with my friends. I don't have the same level of game I used to have, but I'm good enough to hit the ball on the opposite side of the court somehow.
